# Skills Reference — Complete Guide to Your AI Abilities

This document explains every skill your Sidekick has, with example prompts and tips for getting the best results.

---

## How Skills Work

When you ask your assistant to do something, it automatically picks the right skill. You don't need to say "use the copywriting skill" — just describe what you want and your assistant figures it out.

You can also trigger a skill directly by mentioning the skill name:
> "Use your deep-research skill to..."
> "Apply the humanizer to this..."

## Content & Writing Skills

---

### humanizer
**What it does:** Takes any AI-written text and rewrites it to sound like a real human wrote it. Removes the 21 most common "AI writing patterns" that make content sound robotic.

**When to use:** After writing ANY content — emails, social posts, website copy, blog posts.

**Signs you need it:** Words like "delve", "leverage", "it's important to note", "in conclusion", "I'd be happy to", "game-changer", "transformative"

**Example prompts:**
> "Humanize this email: [paste text]"
> "Make this blog post sound less like AI wrote it"
> "Remove all the AI-isms from this LinkedIn post"

**Tips:**
- Always run humanizer AFTER you create content, not before
- It has a 43-word replacement table for the most common AI phrases
- Also removes excessive exclamation marks and hollow phrases

---

### copywriting
**What it does:** Writes persuasive marketing copy using proven frameworks (AIDA, PAS, features-to-benefits). For websites, landing pages, ads, proposals.

**When to use:** When you need to write content that makes people take action (buy, sign up, contact you).

**Example prompts:**
> "Write homepage copy for my [business type]. Our main offer is [offer]. Target customer is [customer]."
> "Write a landing page for my [service] priced at $[price]. The main pain point we solve is [pain]."
> "Rewrite my about page — here's the current version: [paste]"

**Tips:**
- Include your target customer description for much better results
- Specify the action you want people to take
- Ask for multiple versions: "Give me 3 different headline options"

---

### avoid-ai-writing
**What it does:** Audits any piece of content and identifies AI writing patterns, then rewrites them with specific plain-English alternatives. More detailed than humanizer.

**When to use:** When you want a deep audit of content with specific explanations of what's wrong.

**Example prompts:**
> "Audit this blog post and flag every AI-sounding phrase: [paste]"
> "Rewrite this removing all 21 categories of AI patterns"

---

### social-content
**What it does:** Creates social media content for LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok. Includes hooks, content calendars, hashtag strategy.

**When to use:** When you need regular social media posts.

**Example prompts:**
> "Write 7 LinkedIn posts for next week. My business: [describe]. Audience: [describe]. Mix of: tips, stories, and one promotional post."
> "Create an Instagram content calendar for the next 30 days for my [business]"
> "Write a Facebook post about [topic] for [audience]. Casual tone, under 150 words."

**Tips:**
- Give your business niche and audience for much better content
- Ask for different content types: educational, story, promotional, engagement question
- Specify platform — LinkedIn tone is very different to Facebook

---

### content-creator
**What it does:** Creates long-form SEO-optimised content — blog posts, guides, articles, case studies.

**When to use:** When you need content that ranks in Google or positions you as an expert.

**Example prompts:**
> "Write a 1200-word blog post: '5 Ways [Business Type] Can Use AI to Save 10 Hours Per Week'"
> "Create a case study for this client result: [describe the result]"
> "Write a lead magnet guide: 'The Ultimate Guide to [Topic] for [Audience]'"

---

### email-sequence
**What it does:** Builds automated email sequences — welcome series, nurture campaigns, re-engagement campaigns, post-purchase sequences.

**When to use:** When you want to automate how you communicate with leads and customers.

**Example prompts:**
> "Build a 5-email welcome sequence for new leads to my [business]. Each email under 200 words."
> "Write a 3-email re-engagement sequence for contacts who haven't replied in 30 days"
> "Create a post-purchase sequence for customers who just bought my [product/service]"

**Tips:**
- Specify the timing between emails: "Day 0, Day 2, Day 5, Day 10..."
- Include a clear goal for each email
- Humanize the sequence after it's written

## Research Skills

---

### deep-research
**What it does:** Conducts multi-step research using multiple sources — synthesises findings into structured reports.

**When to use:** Market research, competitor research, strategy research, understanding a topic.

**Example prompts:**
> "Research the top 3 competitors in [your industry] — what they offer, pricing, strengths, weaknesses"
> "Research the biggest challenges faced by [your target customer] in 2025"
> "Research the AI automation industry in Australia — market size, trends, key players"

**Tips:**
- Ask for a specific format: "Give me a summary table, then detailed notes"
- Use it before writing content: "Research this topic first, then write the blog post"

---

### reddit-insights
**What it does:** Mines Reddit communities for genuine conversations about your industry, customers' pain points, objections, and desires.

**When to use:** When you want to know what your customers actually think (not curated marketing speak).

**Example prompts:**
> "Search Reddit for what [your target customer type] complain about most"
> "Find Reddit discussions about [competitor name] — what do people love and hate?"
> "What are people on Reddit saying about [industry trend]?"

**Tips:**
- Reddit is gold for finding real customer language to use in your copy
- Ask it to summarise the top themes and exact phrases people use

---

### youtube-summarizer
**What it does:** Fetches a YouTube video's transcript and creates a structured summary with key points, timestamps, and actionable insights.

**When to use:** When you want to learn from a video without watching all of it.

**Example prompts:**
> "Summarise this YouTube video and pull out the 5 most actionable points: [URL]"
> "What does this video say about [topic]? [URL]"
> "Create a written guide based on this YouTube tutorial: [URL]"

---

### research-analyst
**What it does:** Deep landscape analysis — market sizing, competitive positioning, industry trends, evidence-based recommendations.

**When to use:** When you need a proper research report for strategy decisions.

**Example prompts:**
> "Give me a full market research report on [industry] in Australia"
> "Analyse the competitive landscape for [your type of business] in [your city/region]"

---

### competitor-alternatives
**What it does:** Analyses competitor websites, creates comparison content, and identifies positioning opportunities.

**When to use:** When you want to understand where you fit in the market vs competitors.

**Example prompts:**
> "Analyse [competitor website URL] — what they offer, pricing, messaging, and weaknesses"
> "Create a comparison page: [Your Business] vs [Competitor]"
> "Find the gaps in [competitor]'s offering that I could position against"

## Strategy & Sales Skills

---

### brainstorming
**What it does:** Structured ideation — generates large numbers of ideas using proven frameworks, then filters and prioritises them.

**When to use:** Anytime you're stuck or need fresh ideas.

**Example prompts:**
> "Brainstorm 15 ways I could generate more leads for my [business] this month"
> "Give me 20 content ideas for my [business] that would resonate with [audience]"
> "Brainstorm 10 ways I could automate or systemise my client onboarding"

**Tips:**
- Ask for more ideas than you need, then filter: "Give me 20, then pick the top 5"
- Add constraints: "Low-cost only" / "Can be done this week" / "Online only"

---

### sales-automator
**What it does:** Drafts cold emails, follow-ups, proposals, and sales scripts. Includes objection handling.

**When to use:** When you need sales copy or templates.

**Example prompts:**
> "Write a cold email to a [type of business owner] offering [your service]. Casual, under 120 words, end with a question."
> "Write 3 follow-up messages for leads who haven't replied in 1 week"
> "Draft a proposal for a [type] client. Our offer is [describe]. Budget: $[amount]."

---

### writing-plans
**What it does:** Creates detailed plans BEFORE executing complex tasks — maps out steps, risks, and what success looks like.

**When to use:** Before any complex task to make sure the approach is right.

**Example prompts:**
> "Plan how I could automate my lead follow-up process. Map out all the steps."
> "Create a 90-day marketing plan for my business"
> "Plan the steps to launch a new service offering"

---

### prompt-engineer
**What it does:** Improves your AI prompts to get dramatically better results. Teaches you the patterns that make prompts work.

**When to use:** When AI results aren't as good as you hoped.

**Example prompts:**
> "Help me write a better prompt for [task I'm trying to do]"
> "Why isn't this prompt working well, and how can I improve it: [paste prompt]"

---

## Problem-Solving Skills

---

### systematic-debugging
**What it does:** Diagnoses errors and problems step by step. Translates technical errors into plain English and provides a fix.

**When to use:** When ANYTHING breaks.

**Example prompts:**
> "Something broke. Here's the error: [paste the red text]. What does it mean and how do I fix it?"
> "My [tool/app] stopped working. Here's what happened: [describe]. Help me fix it."

**Tips:**
- Always paste the exact error message (the red text) — don't describe it, paste it
- Say what you were doing when it broke
